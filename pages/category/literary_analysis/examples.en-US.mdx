## The Symbolism in "The Great Gatsby"

This essay explores the intricate symbolism F. Scott Fitzgerald employs throughout "The Great Gatsby" to enhance the themes of ambition, love, and the American Dream, emphasizing the green light, the eyes of Dr. T.J. Eckleburg, and more.

## The Role of Social Class in "Pride and Prejudice"

An analysis of how Jane Austen addresses social class and its implications in "Pride and Prejudice," focusing on the relationships and social mobility of characters like Elizabeth Bennet and Mr. Darcy.

## Isolation and Alienation in "Frankenstein"

This essay examines the themes of isolation and alienation in Mary Shelley's "Frankenstein," focusing on Victor Frankenstein and his creation, and how these themes contribute to the tragic outcomes in the story.

## The Use of Foreshadowing in "Macbeth"

An exploration of how William Shakespeare uses foreshadowing to build tension and anticipate the tragic events in "Macbeth," analyzing scenes where the supernatural and prophetic elements are introduced.

## The Theme of Identity in "The Catcher in the Rye"

This essay delves into J.D. Salinger's portrayal of adolescent identity crises in "The Catcher in the Rye," focusing on Holden Caulfield’s journey and his struggle with the concept of growing up.

## Power Dynamics in "1984"

An analysis of George Orwell’s portrayal of power and control in "1984," exploring the mechanisms used by the totalitarian regime to manipulate truth and maintain dominance over the individuals in society.

## Heroism and Tragedy in "Beowulf"

This essay examines the dual themes of heroism and tragedy in the epic poem "Beowulf," analyzing Beowulf’s battles and the ultimate demise, highlighting the cultural values of heroism and mortality.

## The Impact of War in "All Quiet on the Western Front"

An exploration of Erich Maria Remarque’s depiction of war and its brutal realities in "All Quiet on the Western Front," focusing on the psychological and physical toll on soldiers during World War I.

## The Concept of Freedom in "The Adventures of Huckleberry Finn"

This analysis looks at how Mark Twain addresses freedom versus civilization in "The Adventures of Huckleberry Finn," focusing on Huck's adventures and his internal conflicts regarding society's expectations.

## Parallels between "Dracula" and Gothic Literature

An essay dissecting how Bram Stoker’s "Dracula" fits within the gothic literature tradition, discussing elements such as setting, atmosphere, and themes of horror and romance.

## Themes of Revenge in "Hamlet"

An exploration of the themes of revenge in "Hamlet" by William Shakespeare, analyzing how the motivations and actions of different characters converge to create the tragic narrative.

## Gender Inequality in "The Handmaid's Tale"

## Reality vs. Illusion in "A Streetcar Named Desire"

This analysis explores how Tennessee Williams depicts the conflict between reality and illusion in "A Streetcar Named Desire," focusing on the character of Blanche DuBois.

## Quest for Identity in "Invisible Man"

An exploration of Ralph Ellison's "Invisible Man" that examines the protagonist's quest for identity within a racially divided society, focusing on the themes of invisibility and individuality.

This essay analyzes the concept of the tragic hero in Sophocles’ "Oedipus Rex," discussing Oedipus's tragic flaws and fate as central elements to the play’s enduring impact.
