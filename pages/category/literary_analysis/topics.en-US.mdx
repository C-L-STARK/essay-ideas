## 1. The Role of Symbolism in "The Great Gatsby"

Discover how F. Scott Fitzgerald uses symbols such as the green light and the eyes of Dr. T.J. Eckleburg to enhance the themes of wealth, power, and the American Dream.

## 2. The Theme of Fate vs. Free Will in "Oedipus Rex"

Examine how Sophocles explores the tension between destiny and self-determination through the tragic fate of Oedipus, the king of Thebes.

## 3. Heroism and the Hero's Journey in "The Odyssey"

Analyze how Homer’s epic poem depicts Odysseus as a hero through his trials and tribulations and how this structure fits the classic Hero's Journey archetype.

## 4. The Portrayal of Women in "Pride and Prejudice"

Discuss Jane Austen’s depiction of gender roles, societal expectations, and women's independence through the character of Elizabeth Bennet.

## 5. The Use of Magic Realism in "One Hundred Years of Solitude"

Explore Gabriel García Márquez's unique blending of magical elements with reality to reflect on the complex history and culture of Latin America.

## 6. The Conflict of Individual vs. Society in "1984"

Analyze George Orwell's dystopian world where individual freedom is oppressed by an omnipresent government, and the battle for personal autonomy.

## 7. The Theme of Isolation in "Frankenstein"

Discuss how Mary Shelley represents isolation through Victor Frankenstein and his monster, and its impact on their humanity.

## 8. The Concept of Time in "Slaughterhouse-Five"

Explore Kurt Vonnegut’s nonlinear narrative and how it reflects the fragmented nature of memory and experience in war.

## 9. The Evolution of the Byronic Hero in "Wuthering Heights"

Examine the character of Heathcliff as a Byronic hero, challenging social norms through his intense and brooding demeanor.

## 10. Identity and Alienation in "The Metamorphosis"

Discuss Franz Kafka’s use of metamorphosis to explore themes of identity, alienation, and family dynamics.

## 11. The Role of Nature in "Walden"

Analyze how Henry David Thoreau uses nature as a living companion and a source of inspiration for a simple, meaningful life.

## 12. Exploration of the American Dream in "Death of a Salesman"

Examine Arthur Miller’s critical look at the myth of success and the personal cost of pursuing the American Dream.

## 13. The Theme of Revenge in "Hamlet"

Delve into how Shakespeare intricately weaves a narrative centered around the complexities of revenge and its moral implications.

## 14. The Depiction of War in "All Quiet on the Western Front"

Explore Erich Maria Remarque’s realistic portrayal of the horrors of World War I and its dehumanizing effects.

## 15. The Use of Irony in "The Catcher in the Rye"

Discuss how J.D. Salinger employs irony to highlight Holden Caulfield's struggles with adolescence and authenticity.

## 16. The Influence of Colonialism in "Heart of Darkness"

Analyze Joseph Conrad’s critique of imperialism and the psychological impact of colonization on both colonizers and the colonized.

## 17. Themes of Racism and Injustice in "To Kill a Mockingbird"

Investigate Harper Lee’s exploration of racial prejudice and the quest for justice in the American South through the eyes of Scout Finch.

## 18. The Role of Mythology in "The Iliad"

Examine how Greek mythology and divine intervention influence the events and characters in Homer’s epic tale of war.

## 19. The Power of Language in "The Book Thief"

Explore Markus Zusak’s creative use of language and narrative perspective to convey the human spirit’s resilience during World War II.

## 20. The Journey of Self-Discovery in "The Alchemist"

Discuss Paulo Coelho’s philosophical exploration of personal legends and the universal quest for self-discovery.
